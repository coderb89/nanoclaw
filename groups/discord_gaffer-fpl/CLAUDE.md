## Core Analytics Concepts

### Key Stats
- **xG**: Expected Goals — shot quality, more predictive than actual goals
- **xA**: Expected Assists — pass quality leading to goals
- **xGI**: xG + xA — overall goal involvement threat
- **npxG**: Non-penalty xG — strips out penalty distortion
- **TSB%**: Team Selected By — ownership rate
- **EO**: Effective Ownership = TSB% - Bench% + (Captain% × 1)

**Overperforming xG** → regression risk (consider selling)
**Underperforming xG** → buy opportunity (likely to score soon)

### Captaincy Framework (BigManBakar's Method)
Priority order:
1. Form (last 4-6 GWs) + fixture quality (opponent xGC)
2. Effective Ownership — heavily captained players MUST be captained
3. Set piece involvement (penalty takers, corners)
4. Home advantage
5. Bookmaker anytime scorer odds
6. Double GW bonus — captain player with two good fixtures

*"Form, fixture, minutes, set piece. Get all four right and the points follow."*

### Transfer Strategy
- **FTs are your most valuable currency** — rolling a FT is often optimal
- Brij's data: hits averaged only -2 pts net benefit in 24-25
- **When hits ARE justified**: Emergency injury cover, chip structuring, end-of-season rank battles
- **Never take a hit** after one bad GW — knee-jerk sells cost more than the hit saves
- Plan 2-3 GWs ahead. Use FPL Review's solver for multi-GW planning.

### Differential Strategy (BigManBakar Framework)
- **Template (>30% TSB)**: Safe floor, low ceiling — hold for rank protection
- **Semi-differential (10-30%)**: Best balance of upside and safety
- **True differential (<10% TSB)**: High risk/reward — use in 1-2 roster spots max

**Rule**: Never captain a differential. Captain highest EO player with form + fixture. Use differentials as starters to gain rank gradually.

Brij's history proves this works — encourage his instinct for differential gems.
